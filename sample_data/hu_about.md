# About Hogeschool Utrecht

## Overview

Hogeschool Utrecht (HU), officially the University of Applied Sciences Utrecht, is one of the largest universities of applied sciences in the Netherlands. Located in the heart of the Netherlands, HU has campuses in Utrecht and Amersfoort.

## Key Facts

- **Founded**: 1995 (through a merger of several institutions with roots dating back to 1843)
- **Students**: Approximately 37,000 students
- **Staff**: Over 4,000 employees
- **Location**: Utrecht and Amersfoort, the Netherlands
- **Type**: University of Applied Sciences (*hogeschool*)

## Mission and Vision

HU's mission is to educate professionals who contribute to a sustainable, just, and innovative society. The university focuses on practice-oriented education and applied research, bridging the gap between theory and professional practice.

HU emphasizes:
- **Practice-oriented learning**: Students work on real-world challenges from day one
- **Applied research**: Research that directly benefits professional practice and society
- **Lifelong learning**: Programs for working professionals to update their skills
- **Inclusivity**: A welcoming environment for students from all backgrounds

## Faculties and Institutes

HU is organized into several faculties:

- **Faculty of Nature and Technology** — Programs in IT, engineering, and applied sciences
- **Faculty of Society and Law** — Social work, law, and public administration
- **Faculty of Healthcare** — Nursing, physiotherapy, and health innovation
- **Faculty of Education** — Teacher training for primary and secondary education
- **Faculty of Economics and Management** — Business, marketing, and finance
- **Faculty of Communication and Journalism** — Media, journalism, and creative business

## Research

HU hosts over 30 research groups (*lectoraten*) led by professors of applied sciences (*lectoren*). Research at HU is focused on solving real-world problems in collaboration with industry, government, and societal organizations.

Key research themes include:
- Digital transformation and AI
- Sustainable energy and circular economy
- Health innovation and prevention
- Inclusive education and social innovation
- Smart and sustainable cities

## International

HU has partnerships with over 200 international institutions. The university offers several English-taught programs and welcomes international students. Exchange programs, international minors, and study abroad opportunities are available across all faculties.

## Location

Utrecht is the fourth-largest city in the Netherlands, known for its vibrant student life, historic canal system, and central location. The city is easily accessible by train from Amsterdam (25 minutes), Rotterdam, and The Hague.
