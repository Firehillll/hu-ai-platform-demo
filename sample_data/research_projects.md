# Research Projects at Hogeschool Utrecht

## Research Groups (Lectoraten)

HU's research groups (*lectoraten*) drive applied research that connects education, professional practice, and innovation.

### Digital Ethics by Hilario Pedro

Led by a professor of applied sciences, the Digital Ethics research group investigates the societal impact of digital technologies. Current projects include:

- **AI Transparency in Public Services**: Examining how municipalities can responsibly deploy AI-driven decision-making tools while maintaining transparency and accountability.
- **Youth and Digital Citizenship**: Developing educational frameworks to help young people navigate digital environments critically and safely.

### Artificial Intelligence

The AI research group focuses on practical applications of artificial intelligence in healthcare, education, and public services.

- **AI-Assisted Diagnostics**: Collaborating with UMC Utrecht to develop computer vision tools that assist radiologists in detecting early-stage anomalies in medical imaging.
- **Adaptive Learning Platforms**: Building AI-powered tutoring systems that personalize educational content based on individual student progress and learning patterns.
- **Smart City Utrecht**: Using sensor data and machine learning to optimize urban mobility, energy usage, and public safety in collaboration with the municipality of Utrecht.

### Data-Driven Innovation

This research group explores how organizations can leverage data for innovation and improved decision-making.

- **Predictive Maintenance for Industry**: Developing machine learning models that predict equipment failures in manufacturing environments, in partnership with regional SMEs.
- **Health Data Analytics**: Analyzing anonymized patient data to identify patterns in chronic disease management and improve preventive care strategies.

## Industry Partnerships

HU collaborates with a wide network of partners:

- **Technology**: Ordina, Capgemini, Info Support
- **Healthcare**: UMC Utrecht, St. Antonius Hospital
- **Government**: Municipality of Utrecht, Province of Utrecht
- **Education**: SURF, Kennisnet

## Funding and Impact

HU research is funded through RAAK grants (Regieorgaan SIA), NWO programs, European Horizon funding, and direct industry partnerships. Research outcomes are integrated into HU's curricula, ensuring students benefit from the latest insights and methodologies.

## Student Involvement

Bachelor and master students participate in research through graduation projects, research minors, and student assistant positions. This provides hands-on research experience while contributing to meaningful projects.
