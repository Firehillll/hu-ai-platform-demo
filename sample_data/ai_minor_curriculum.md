# Applied Artificial Intelligence Minor

## Overview

The Applied AI minor at Hogeschool Utrecht is a 30 ECTS semester program designed for students who want to develop practical skills in artificial intelligence. The minor combines theoretical foundations with hands-on projects, preparing students to apply AI in their professional domain.

## Prerequisites

- Completed at least 90 ECTS in a bachelor program
- Basic programming experience (Python preferred)
- Introductory knowledge of mathematics and statistics

## Semester Structure

### Block 1: Foundations (15 ECTS)

**Machine Learning Fundamentals (5 ECTS)**
- Supervised and unsupervised learning algorithms
- Model training, evaluation, and validation
- Feature engineering and data preprocessing
- Tools: scikit-learn, pandas, numpy

**Natural Language Processing (5 ECTS)**
- Text preprocessing and representation
- Sentiment analysis and text classification
- Introduction to transformers and large language models
- Tools: spaCy, Hugging Face Transformers

**Ethics and Responsible AI (5 ECTS)**
- EU AI Act and regulatory frameworks
- Bias detection and fairness in AI systems
- Privacy, transparency, and explainability
- Societal impact assessment of AI applications

### Block 2: Applied AI (15 ECTS)

**Computer Vision (5 ECTS)**
- Image classification and object detection
- Convolutional neural networks
- Transfer learning and fine-tuning
- Tools: PyTorch, OpenCV

**AI Project (10 ECTS)**
- Team-based project with an industry partner
- Full AI development lifecycle: problem definition, data collection, model development, deployment
- Agile methodology and professional communication
- Deliverables: working prototype, technical report, presentation

## Learning Outcomes

Upon completion, students can:
1. Design and implement machine learning solutions for real-world problems
2. Evaluate AI systems for bias, fairness, and ethical compliance
3. Apply NLP and computer vision techniques in professional contexts
4. Work effectively in multidisciplinary AI project teams
5. Communicate AI concepts to both technical and non-technical audiences

## Assessment

- Individual assignments and exams (40%)
- Team project deliverables (40%)
- Portfolio and reflection (20%)
